"The Father of Computer Science"

Widely regarded as the "Father of Computer Science", Alan Turing was a mathmatician and codebreaker who was heavily involved in the development of the first electronic computers.

During the Second World War, Turing was part of the British code-breaking efforts at Bletchley Park, whose work involved decrypting the German Enigma Code.

After the war Turing continued his work in Computing and helped develop the subject. Sadly, Turing was persecuted for his homosexuality and sentenced to chemical treatments. He died soon after.

Turing was a noted athlete, especially in running

Below is a timeline of important events in his life:

  • 1912 - Born in London
  • 1926 - Starts at Sherborne School, Dorset
  • 1931 - Begins his undergraduate study at King's College, Cambridge
  • 1936 - Publishes the paper "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem", laying down the foundation of computation
  • 1938 - Receives his PHD from Princeton University
  • 1939 - Begins working at Bletchley Park
  • 1946 - Presents the first detailed design of the stored-program computer
  • 1950 - Begins formulating the early theories of Artificial Intelligence
  • 1952 - Convicted of "Gross Indecency" and sentenced to chemical castration
  • 1954 - Dies at the age of 41

The Father of Computer Science

Widely regarded as the "Father of Computer Science", Alan Turing was a mathmatician and codebreaker who was heavily involved in the development of the first electronic computers.

During the Second World War, Turing was part of the British code-breaking efforts at Bletchley Park, whose work involved decrypting the German Enigma Code.

After the war Turing continued his work in Computing and helped develop the subject. Sadly, Turing was persecuted for his homosexuality and sentenced to chemical treatments. He died soon after.
